Hair loss is very common. 25% percent of men will start experiencing some hair loss before age 21 and two-thirds of men will experience noticeable hair loss before age 35. For women, 12% experience hair loss in their 20s, while 50% of women experience hair loss over their lifetime. With many people being affected, it’s no surprise that the hair loss treatment industry is estimated to be worth three billion dollars today.

We see shelves full of hair-loss treatment products in pharmacies with the most popular being minoxidil or more commonly known as Rogaine. Minoxidil is over-the-counter, heavily advertised and is the product that most people use when they experience hair loss. Despite it’s popularity however, it is not the most effective treatment.

A recent study that combined 33 clinical trials ranked hair loss treatments. The study used a method called network meta-analysis, which allows the comparison of treatments even if they were not tested directly. The results showed dutasteride as the most effective with an effectiveness score of 96.3%. Finasteride ranked second with a score of 87.1%. Minoxidil (Rogaine) ranked third with a score of 52.4%.

This makes sense if we consider that pattern baldness is driven by a hormone called DHT. Dutasteride reduces DHT by about 92% and finasteride reduces DHT by about 73%. Minoxidil works differently in that it increases blood flow to the hair follicles, which helps but does not address the root cause of the problem.

The study also found that oral medications are more effective than topical medication of the same drug (the case with topical finasteride), which is another reason why topical medicines are note as effective as advertised.

Although not the most effective treatment, minoxidil is still heavily advertised. This is mainly due of its accessibility. Because minoxidil is over-the-counter, companies are able to directly market to consumers. To compare, dutasteride and finasteride have to be prescribed and so are not advertised to the same degree which makes it harder to buy/become informed about. The result is a gap between what’s most marketed and what’s most effective.

For anyone considering treatment, the takeaway is simple. The products that you see advertised most aren’t necessarily the most effective. Having a conversation with a doctor about your options, especially prescription options like dutasteride or finasteride, might be worth the effort if you’re serious about results. This is not medical advice.
